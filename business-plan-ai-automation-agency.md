# Business Plan: AI Automation Agency → Vertical SaaS

## Executive summary

Build a cash-flowing AI automation agency for small service businesses, then productize the repeated workflow into vertical SaaS.

The goal is not to chase a novel startup idea. The goal is to find a painful, repeated business process that already costs companies money, sell a done-for-you fix, document the repeated delivery steps, and convert the repeatable parts into software.

Initial wedge: fast lead follow-up and booking automation for local service businesses.

Target outcome:

- 90 days: 5 paying clients, ~$7,500/month recurring revenue plus setup fees
- 12 months: $25k–$75k/month revenue from productized services
- 24 months: transition strongest workflow into SaaS or sell/scale the agency

---

## 1. Market opportunity

Small businesses are drowning in operational drag:

- missed leads
- slow follow-up
- messy inboxes
- manual scheduling
- inconsistent CRM usage
- no reporting discipline
- repetitive document/admin work

Most owners do not want “AI.” They want more booked calls, fewer missed opportunities, less admin, and more predictable revenue.

This creates a strong opportunity for a practical operator-style service: install simple automation that directly improves revenue or saves labor.

---

## 2. Target customer

Start with one niche. Do not sell broadly.

Best early niches:

1. Med spas
2. Dental offices
3. Law firms
4. Real estate teams
5. Insurance agencies
6. Home services companies
7. High-ticket coaches/consultants

Recommended first niche: **med spas**.

Why:

- They buy leads from Instagram, ads, referrals, and websites.
- Speed-to-lead matters.
- Missed inquiries directly cost revenue.
- Treatments have high ticket value.
- Many are owner-operated and operationally messy.
- They are reachable through Instagram, local search, and paid ads research.

## 3. Core offer

### Offer name

**60-Second Lead Capture System**

### Promise

“We install an AI-assisted follow-up system that responds to new leads within 60 seconds, qualifies them, books consultations, and updates your CRM automatically.”

### Business outcome

- more booked consultations
- fewer missed leads
- faster response time
- cleaner pipeline tracking
- less manual admin

### Pricing

Starter:

- $2,500 setup
- $750/month support + optimization

Growth:

- $5,000 setup
- $1,500/month support + reporting

Premium:

- $10,000 setup
- $2,500/month including custom workflows, SMS, dashboards, and monthly optimization

### Guarantee

“If we do not recover or generate at least $5,000 in qualified pipeline in 60 days, you do not pay the second month.”

---

## 4. Productized service scope

Initial workflow:

1. Capture new leads from website forms, Instagram, Facebook, ads, email, and missed calls.
2. Send instant response through email/SMS.
3. Ask qualifying questions.
4. Route qualified leads to booking calendar.
5. Notify staff of high-value leads.
6. Update CRM or spreadsheet.
7. Send daily/weekly lead report.
8. Trigger follow-up sequence for non-responders.

Do not overbuild. The first version can run on existing tools.

---

## 5. Delivery stack

Use off-the-shelf tools before building custom software.

Recommended stack:

- Make, Zapier, or n8n for automation
- Airtable or Google Sheets for lightweight CRM/reporting
- Gmail / Google Calendar
- Twilio or HighLevel for SMS
- OpenAI / Claude / Gemini for AI responses and classification
- Tally / Typeform / website forms for intake
- Looker Studio or Airtable Interfaces for dashboards

Rule: do not build SaaS until the same workflow has been sold and delivered at least 5–10 times.

## 6. Customer acquisition

### Daily outbound target

Send 50 targeted messages per day.

### Channels

- Instagram DMs
- cold email
- LinkedIn
- Google Maps prospecting
- local business associations
- niche Facebook groups
- referrals from first client

### Prospect criteria

Look for businesses that:

- run ads
- post actively on social
- have online booking
- offer high-ticket services
- have slow or weak follow-up
- have poor reviews mentioning communication delays

### Cold message

“Hey — noticed you’re getting leads through Instagram/your site, but most clinics lose money when follow-up takes more than a few minutes. I build a 60-second follow-up system that captures, qualifies, and books consults automatically. Want me to show you what it would look like for [business name]?”

### Follow-up

Day 2:

“Quick follow-up — this is mainly useful if missed leads or slow response time are costing you booked consults. If not, no worries.”

Day 5:

“I can make a quick mockup using your current intake flow so you can see the exact system before deciding. Worth sending?”

## 7. Sales process

### Discovery questions

- Where do new leads come from?
- How fast do you usually respond?
- How many leads go unanswered?
- What is a booked consultation worth?
- What is a new customer worth?
- Who handles follow-up now?
- What CRM/calendar do you use?
- What happens when someone does not respond?

### Sales math

If a client gets 100 leads/month and 20 are missed or poorly followed up, recovering even 3–5 clients can pay for the system.

Example:

- average customer value: $1,000
- recovered customers/month: 5
- recovered revenue: $5,000/month
- system cost: $1,500/month
- ROI: 3.3x before staff time savings

## 8. 90-day execution plan

### Days 1–7: choose niche and validate pain

- Pick one niche.
- Build a list of 200 prospects.
- Interview 10–20 owners/operators.
- Identify the most common missed-money workflow.
- Create one-page offer.

### Days 8–30: sell first clients

- Send 50 targeted messages/day.
- Book discovery calls.
- Close 1–2 beta clients.
- Deliver manually if needed.
- Track before/after metrics.

### Days 31–60: standardize delivery

- Turn setup into checklists.
- Create reusable automation templates.
- Document every repeated step.
- Collect testimonials/case studies.
- Raise pricing for new clients.

### Days 61–90: scale acquisition

- Close 3–5 more clients.
- Hire a contractor for implementation support.
- Build a basic internal dashboard.
- Identify which workflow repeats enough to become SaaS.

90-day target:

- 5 clients
- $7,500/month recurring revenue
- $10k–$25k in setup fees collected
- repeatable delivery process documented

## 11. Risks and mitigations

### Risk: selling vague AI automation

Mitigation: sell a concrete business outcome, not AI.

### Risk: custom work destroys margin

Mitigation: one niche, one workflow, reusable templates.

### Risk: clients churn after setup

Mitigation: monthly reporting, optimization, lead monitoring, and ongoing improvements.

### Risk: integrations are messy

Mitigation: start with simple systems; charge more for complex integrations.

### Risk: no product emerges

Mitigation: productize only after repeated delivery patterns are proven.

---

## 12. Path to getting rich

Stage 1: cash-flowing service

- Sell setup + retainer.
- Generate immediate income.
- Learn the market from real clients.

Stage 2: productized agency

- Narrow the offer.
- Build templates.
- Hire delivery help.
- Scale to $25k–$75k/month.

Stage 3: vertical SaaS

- Turn repeated workflow into software.
- Keep agency clients as first users.
- Charge subscription fees.

Stage 4: compound or exit

- Keep collecting high-margin recurring revenue.
- Sell the agency/SaaS.
- Roll profits into other cash-flowing assets.
