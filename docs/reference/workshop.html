Workshop

Creativity, Judgment, and Reclaiming Human Expertise in the Age of AI

A three-hour participatory workshop exploring creativity, cognition, and collective thinking in the age of AI.

About the workshop

AI can help us write faster, brainstorm faster, synthesize faster, and make decisions faster. But what happens to our creative process when answers become instant?

As designers, researchers, and creative practitioners, we’re among the first people learning how to work alongside AI every day. Yet few of us have had the opportunity to step back and reflect on what that relationship might be doing to the way we think.

Through discussion, reflection, and collaborative activities, we’ll examine questions that rarely fit into product roadmaps, conference talks, or LinkedIn posts.

Questions we’ll explore

  • Where does AI genuinely help us think better?
  • Where does it risk replacing thinking altogether?
  • What kinds of creative friction are worth preserving?
  • How does collaboration change when AI enters the room?
  • What parts of thinking are worth protecting?

What’s included

  • A 3-hour facilitated workshop
  • Breakfast, coffee, and snacks
  • Interactive exercises and group discussion
  • A collective artifact created by participants
  • Thoughtful conversations with fellow designers and researchers
  • No preparation required — just bring your curiosity

Facilitator

Iris Latour

Director of Research at THEFT Studio, where she researches AI products, AI transformation, and the evolving relationship between people and intelligent systems.

She holds a Master’s degree in Design for Responsible AI from Elisava and has spent her career helping organizations turn complex human behavior into meaningful product decisions.

“It’s not an anti-AI workshop. It’s not a productivity workshop. And it’s definitely not another prompt engineering session.”
